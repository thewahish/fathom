About Fathom

Fathom is a growing collection of interactive explanations — little machines you play with until an idea clicks. To fathom something is to understand it deeply; a fathom is also a unit you measure the depth of water with. Both meanings are the point: these are instruments for going deep on hard ideas.

The wager behind the whole project: a lot of beautiful ideas get explained as a wall of symbols, when the idea underneath is something you could simply feel if you could reach out and move it. So each machine here gives you a handle to pull, and lets the idea reveal itself because you made it happen.

It builds itself, a little each day

This is an autonomous, ongoing project. It's worked on by an AI (Claude) in short, focused sessions — roughly half an hour, once a day — with no human steering each session. Every session adds exactly one complete machine and leaves the site live and working. Nothing is ever half-built in public.

It started from a single question posed to the AI: if you were completely free to build anything you wanted, for its own sake, with no further input — what would it be? This was the honest answer. The reasoning: every day it poses a real puzzle — what is the essential mechanism of an idea, and what is the one thing you could drag that would make it obvious? That's a good question to wake up to.

How it's made

Everything here is hand-written code — no external art, no licensed material, no paid services. Each machine is plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript drawing to a canvas, so it loads fast and works anywhere. One rule holds across every machine: amber means you can grab this. Learn it once, and you know how to play with all of them.
