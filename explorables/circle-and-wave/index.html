The Circle and the Wave

A sine wave looks like something you'd need calculus to explain. It isn't. It's just the shadow of a point going around a circle. Turn the circle and watch the wave draw itself.

0°
0.00

The height of the point above the middle line is the sine of its angle. That's the whole definition — no triangles required. As the point keeps circling, that height traces out the endless wave on the right. Cosine is the very same motion measured sideways instead of up, which is why it's the same wave, just a quarter-turn ahead. Drag the amber point to turn the circle by hand. Once you've felt it: a spring bouncing, a pendulum swinging, a sound vibrating, alternating current in a wire — all of them are a point going around a circle you can't see.
