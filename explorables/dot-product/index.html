The Dot Product, Seen as a Shadow

"Multiply the vectors" sounds abstract until you see what it actually measures: how much one arrow falls along the other's direction, like a shadow cast straight down onto it. Drag either tip and watch the shadow — and the number — move.

Drop a perpendicular from the tip of B straight onto the line through A — that's B's shadow on A, and its signed length is |B|·cos(angle). The dot product is just that shadow's length times how long A is: A·B = |A| × (B's shadow on A). When the angle between them is under 90°, the shadow falls forward, on A's own side of the origin, and A·B is positive. Push the angle past 90° and the shadow flips to fall behind the origin — the shadow, and the dot product, go negative. At exactly 90° the shadow vanishes to a point: two vectors with nothing of each other in them. Drag either amber arrowtip. Watch the shadow (the thick segment on A's line) slide through zero as you swing B across perpendicular.
