No. 03

The Board That Builds a Bell Curve

Every ball that falls through this board makes a string of pure coin-flip decisions — nothing but chance, one peg at a time. Watch what chance does anyway, once enough of it piles up.

50% right
0

Each ball hits a row of pegs and bounces left or right off each one — a fresh, independent coin flip every time, decided purely by the amber lever's odds. One ball's path is unpredictable. But the more rights and lefts a path needs to add up, the more paths there are that land near the middle and the fewer that land at either edge — so the pile that builds at the bottom isn't random at all. It's the bell curve, assembling itself out of nothing but chance. Drag the amber lever off-center and the whole pile leans with it — the shape survives, just shifted. Pull "rows" up and the curve gets smoother and relatively narrower: more coin flips per ball, less relative room for extremes. This is the same reason exam scores, heights, and measurement errors all tend to land in bell curves — each one is the sum of many small, mostly-independent nudges.
